Personal Website
The idea behind this website started with me wanting to showcase my projects and have the ability to document my progress and experiences. As I developed the idea, it turned into a personal portfolio website where I could also showcase my skills and experience. The design and look of the website was the most important part. I wanted to create a website which was eye-catching, modern, and sleek.

I wanted to bolster my design and UX skills, so I used a more formal design process than I normally would for personal projects. There ended up being four iterations of my design process. The first iteration was drawn up on a piece of scrap paper during a workshop when the project first came to me. The second and third were more formal sketches. The final was a prototype made in Figma along with a 3-step action plan. I found this formal planning process to be very useful and helped me achieve my goal in a more focussed and efficient manner. I am very happy with how it has turned out.

Twitter Bot
During the 2021 Delta outbreak lockdown I got bored and starting coming up with fun ideas with different APIs. I came across the twitter API and thought I could create a bot which tweets something once a day. My girlfriend came up with the idea of a bot which compliments her every day. I thought it would be funny to have a template compliment where one word of the words is random each day. I found a random word generator API and linked that into the Twitter API, then scheduled a script to run once a day to post the tweet. You can check it out here.
Unity Game
During my final semester at University I took CompSci 399 where we had to collaborate as a group to create a project. My group decided to develop a video game and post it online for others to play. The game was called "Into the Dark" and was developed in Unity. My main role was the art lead. I created most of the artwork and assets for the game and controlled a lot of the art direction. This was my first experience working on a long term project as a group, and my first experience with Unity. The game took us a full semester to develop. With more time we could've made a longer game, but I am extremely proud of how this turned out. It was the crowning achievement of my degree and had an awesome experience creating it. You can check out the game here.
